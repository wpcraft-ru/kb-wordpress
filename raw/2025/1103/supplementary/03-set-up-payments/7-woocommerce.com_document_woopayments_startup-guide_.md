# WooPayments: Startup Guide

**[Spanish version of this page](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/woopayments-guia-de-inicio/)** | [Aquí puedes encontrar la versión en español de esta página.](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/woopayments-guia-de-inicio/)

Thanks for choosing WooPayments!

We provide an integrated payments solution to get your WooCommerce store up and running quickly without setup costs or monthly fees. With WooPayments, you’ll get:

*   Credit and debit card acceptance
*   Local payment method offerings
*   Automated payouts to your bank account
*   Buy now, pay later payment methods
*   Built-in multi-currency support
*   Easy integration of popular digital wallets

Managing payments — including refunds, disputes, and payouts — is integrated into your site’s WordPress administrator dashboard, so it all happens from within your site’s admin dashboard.

## [Requirements for WooPayments](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/startup-guide/#requirements)

[↑ Back to top](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/startup-guide/#doc-title "Back to top")
First, let’s make sure you have what’s required to ensure a smooth installation:

*   Your business is based in one of our [supported countries](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/compatibility/countries/#supported-countries).
*   PHP, WordPress, and WooCommerce are on recent versions.
*   Your site has [an SSL certificate](https://woocommerce.com/document/ssl-and-https/) and is accessible over HTTPS.
*   You are at least 18 years old.

### [Check for WooPayments](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/startup-guide/#check-for-woopayments)

[↑ Back to top](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/startup-guide/#doc-title "Back to top")
Go to **Plugins > Installed Plugins** to see if WooPayments is already installed. If it is, great! Head to the [Signing up for WooPayments](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/startup-guide/#signing-up) section and continue from there.

If it’s not listed, please follow the instructions in the **Installing WooPayments** section below.

## [Installing WooPayments](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/startup-guide/#installation)

[↑ Back to top](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/startup-guide/#doc-title "Back to top")
Most users will be able to install WooPayments directly from the **WooCommerce > Payments** settings. You can access the Payments settings through any of the following options:

*   Selecting the **Payments** menu item.
*   Selecting the **Set up payments** option in the WooCommerce setup list at **WooCommerce > Home**.
*   Navigating to **WooCommerce > Settings > Payments**.

Once you’ve navigated to the Payments settings, you can select the **Install** button for the **Accept payments with Woo** option in the list.

*   The **Business location** at the top of the page is not set to one of our [supported countries](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/compatibility/countries/#supported-countries).
*   You previously hid the WooPayments suggestion.

In either of these cases, you will need to manually install WooPayments.

### [Before you begin](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/startup-guide/#before-you-begin)

[↑ Back to top](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/startup-guide/#doc-title "Back to top")
Before starting the signup process, keep the following information in mind:

*   Although most of the signup process takes place on your site, there will be a popup shown from `connect.stripe.com` (Stripe is [our payments partner](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/account-management/partnership-with-stripe/)) that collects your phone number and email address. This popup is normal.
*   Be sure to [select the correct country](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/compatibility/countries/) for your account, as it cannot be changed later. If you’re using WooPayments for a business, the relevant country is the one where your business is registered, not the one in which you personally live.
*   You will have the option to create a [test account](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/testing-and-troubleshooting/test-accounts/) as a part of the signup process, which allows you to explore WooPayments using simulated information.

### [Test mode](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/startup-guide/#test-mode)

[↑ Back to top](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/startup-guide/#doc-title "Back to top")
[Test mode](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/testing-and-troubleshooting/testing/) lets you simulate transactions using [test card numbers](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/testing-and-troubleshooting/testing/#test-cards) so that you can ensure your checkout process is working as expected. Simply put any product in the cart and go through the checkout process and the order confirmation page to see exactly what customers would see during an actual purchase.

When you’re ready to go live, remember to disable test mode before accepting live payments.

**NOTE:** Test mode **only** works with test credit card numbers. Using real credit card information while WooPayments is in test mode will cause the order to fail and an error will be displayed.
