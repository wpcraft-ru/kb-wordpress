# WooPayments: Fraud Protection

WooPayments offers configurable fraud protection rules for merchants. This helps you avoid disputes by setting a protection risk level that best suits your business.

This page explains how fraud protection works, what the various settings do, and how they interact with incoming orders from your customers.

## [How fraud protection works](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/fraud-and-disputes/fraud-protection/#how-it-works)

[↑ Back to top](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/fraud-and-disputes/fraud-protection/#doc-title "Back to top")
When a new order is placed, it is first evaluated by the fraud protection rules you’ve configured. Those rules determine if the order will be allowed or blocked.

*   **Allowed:** The customer will be charged and the order will succeed. In short, these orders will behave just the same as orders that use any other payment gateway.
*   **Blocked:** The customer’s payment method is not charged, and the order is set to the Pending Payment status. Optionally, you can [remove these](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/fraud-and-disputes/fraud-protection/#removing-blocked-orders) after a set time.

Allowed orders will be shown in their normal locations, e.g. **Payments > Transactions** and **WooCommerce > Orders**. We have also added a **Blocked** tab to the **Payments > Transactions** page, which shows the orders that were automatically cancelled.

[![Image 8](https://woocommerce.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/Screenshot-taken-on-2025-02-06-at-10.10.12-UTC@2x.png?strip=all&w=704)](https://woocommerce.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/Screenshot-taken-on-2025-02-06-at-10.10.12-UTC@2x.png)

The blocked transactions list.

## [Configuring fraud protection rules](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/fraud-and-disputes/fraud-protection/#configuring-rules)

[↑ Back to top](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/fraud-and-disputes/fraud-protection/#doc-title "Back to top")
Fraud protection rules are configured on the **Payments > Settings** page, in the _Fraud Protection_ section. There are two options:

*   **Basic:** Incoming orders will only be blocked if the customer’s bank cannot verify the card security code against their own data. This is called [the CVC check](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/fraud-and-disputes/card-verification-checks/#cvc-checks).
*   **Advanced:** This option allows you to customize the fraud protection rules as needed.

[![Image 9](https://woocommerce.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/Screenshot-taken-on-2025-04-12-at-13.52.25-UTC@2x.png?strip=all&w=704)](https://woocommerce.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/Screenshot-taken-on-2025-04-12-at-13.52.25-UTC@2x.png)
### [Advanced configuration](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/fraud-and-disputes/fraud-protection/#advanced-configuration)

[↑ Back to top](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/fraud-and-disputes/fraud-protection/#doc-title "Back to top")
If you want more control than the **Basic** risk level offers, you can select the **Advanced** risk level and configure additional rules. The rules you can add are the following:

*   **[AVS Mismatch](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/fraud-and-disputes/card-verification-checks/#avs-checks):** Compares postcode submitted by the customer to the postcode on file with their card’s issuing bank. Orders will be blocked if the two do not match up.
*   **International IP Address:** Blocks the order if the customer’s [IP address](https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/IP_address) is outside [the countries you sell to](https://woocommerce.com/document/configuring-woocommerce-settings/general/), even if the billing country entered _is_ one you sell to.
*   **IP Address Mismatch:** Compares the customer’s billing country to the country that their IP address seems to originate from. Blocks the order if those do not match.
*   **Address Mismatch:** Blocks orders if the billing country and shipping country differ.
*   **Purchase Price Threshold:** Compares the total order price to the minimum and maximum that you’ve allowed. Blocks orders if the total is outside the range.
*   **Order Items Threshold:** Blocks orders if the total number of items in the order is lower than or greater than the range you’ve allowed.

Enabling a rule will block orders if they meet that rule’s conditions. For example, the following configuration will automatically block orders with over 20 products:

[![Image 10](https://woocommerce.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/Screenshot-taken-on-2025-04-29-at-17.40.19-UTC@2x.png?strip=all&w=704)](https://woocommerce.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/Screenshot-taken-on-2025-04-29-at-17.40.19-UTC@2x.png)

Blocking transactions with more than 20 items

The CVC Verification rule at the bottom cannot be disabled. Card payments that fail CVC verification will always be blocked. Please see [this document](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/fraud-and-disputes/card-verification-checks/#cvc-checks) to learn more.

## [Viewing rule results](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/fraud-and-disputes/fraud-protection/#viewing-results)

[↑ Back to top](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/fraud-and-disputes/fraud-protection/#doc-title "Back to top")
Incoming orders are evaluated according to the rules you’ve configured. The outcome is noted on the order details page under **WooCommerce > Orders**. Clicking either of the **View more details** links will take you to the transaction details page.

[![Image 11](https://woocommerce.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/CleanShot-2024-04-30-at-21.09.07.png?strip=all&w=704)](https://woocommerce.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/CleanShot-2024-04-30-at-21.09.07.png)
You can also get to the transaction details page directly, by going to **Payments > Transactions** and clicking a transaction. As noted above, blocked transactions will be added to the **Blocked** tab, instead of the main **Transactions** list.

By clicking a blocked transaction to see its timeline, you can find the fraud protection rule(s) that were triggered for that specific transaction.

[![Image 12](https://woocommerce.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/CleanShot-2024-04-30-at-21.10.43.png?strip=all&w=704)](https://woocommerce.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/CleanShot-2024-04-30-at-21.10.43.png)
## [What customers see](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/fraud-and-disputes/fraud-protection/#what-customers-see)

[↑ Back to top](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/fraud-and-disputes/fraud-protection/#doc-title "Back to top")
In order to avoid revealing your specific fraud protection rules to customers, WooPayments will show a generic error message when the rules block a transaction.

[![Image 13](https://woocommerce.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/Screenshot-taken-on-2025-09-04-at-15.42.53-UTC@2x.png?strip=all&w=704)](https://woocommerce.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/Screenshot-taken-on-2025-09-04-at-15.42.53-UTC@2x.png)
Customers who see this message will be able to retry the order if they wish.

## [Removing blocked transactions](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/fraud-and-disputes/fraud-protection/#removing-blocked-transactions)

[↑ Back to top](https://woocommerce.com/document/woopayments/fraud-and-disputes/fraud-protection/#doc-title "Back to top")
If you wish, you can automatically delete blocked orders after a period of time by configuring the “Retain pending orders” setting under **WooCommerce > Settings > Accounts & Privacy**.

Note, however, that this will affect **all** Pending Payment orders, not just those that were blocked by the WooPayments fraud protection rules.

[![Image 14](https://woocommerce.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/Screenshot-taken-on-2025-02-06-at-10.12.36-UTC@2x.png?strip=all&w=704)](https://woocommerce.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/Screenshot-taken-on-2025-02-06-at-10.12.36-UTC@2x.png)

Setting a retention period for pending orders
